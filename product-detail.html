Frequently Asked Questions

Antivirus software is a type of program designed to protect computers and other devices from malicious software (malware), including viruses, worms, Trojans, and other threats. It works by scanning files and programs for known malware signatures, analyzing behavior patterns, and potentially blocking or removing infected files. Antivirus software often runs in the background to provide real-time protection and is an essential part of a comprehensive cybersecurity strategy.
Malware is a term for any type of malicious software, regardless of how it works, its intent or how itâ€™s distributed.
